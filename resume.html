With it I demonstrated 100% accuracy on a distributed three-qubit Grover's algorithm across simulated networked nodes, and built applications that model quantum teleportation and remote gate operations — work I presented at the 2025 Chicago Quantum Summit.

University of Wisconsin–Madison

Summer 2023 – 2025 · Madison, WI

Quantum Computing Research Assistant — Benchmarking & Quantum Annealing, advised by Prof. Swamit Tannu and Prof. Krishnan Suresh.

I developed benchmark suites with novel metrics for predicting resource expenditure and error rates on fault-tolerant hardware, using variational autoencoders to probe how classically-simulable metrics correlate with real device performance. On the annealing side, I adapted quantum annealing algorithms for inequality-constrained optimization — with applications to finance portfolio problems — improving linear-system solving efficiency by 30–40% and applying the methods to 2D-Poisson, thermal, and topology optimization problems, presented at WCCM Vancouver 2024.
