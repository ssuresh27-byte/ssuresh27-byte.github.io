Science Bowl

Of everything I did in high school, the National Science Bowl shaped me the most. I served as president and captain of my team, and ranked 2nd nationally among individual competitors.

The buzzer

Science Bowl is a fast, buzzer-based quiz competition spanning physics, chemistry, biology, math, earth science, and energy. A question is read, and the first person to recall — and commit — wins the points. You learn to hold a huge amount of material at your fingertips and to make a decision in the half-second before someone else does.

I loved that pressure. It rewarded genuine understanding and quick pattern recognition in equal measure, and it pushed me to go deep across every science at once rather than picking a single lane. A lot of my comfort with physics — and eventually quantum computing — started here, with a buzzer in my hand.

Building a community around it

As captain, the part I'm proudest of wasn't my own scores — it was getting other people into it. I built practice platforms and ran sessions that ended up engaging more than 2,000 students, trying to make the on-ramp into competitive science as welcoming as possible. Watching someone go from intimidated to obsessed was the best part.

How it shaped my life

Science Bowl is the thread that connects most of what I do now. The thrill of fast recall and the urge to make practice better is exactly why I keep building learning tools — Eloquizzer, PDFQuizzer, and a Science Bowl practice app are all, in a sense, attempts to bottle that experience for other students.

It also taught me how to lead a team, how to teach, and how to lose a close match and come back the next round. The friendships and the habits of curiosity I picked up have outlasted any single answer I ever buzzed in.
